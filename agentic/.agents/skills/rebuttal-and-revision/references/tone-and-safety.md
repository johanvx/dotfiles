# Tone and Safety Guide for Rebuttals

## Goals
- Be precise, respectful, and evidence-backed.
- Reduce friction with reviewers by being explicit and concrete.

## Do
- Thank briefly.
- Restate concern accurately.
- State exact revision and where it appears.
- Use conservative, bounded language.
- Admit limitations when unresolved.

## Don’t
- Be defensive, sarcastic, or vague.
- Claim fixes not implemented.
- Invent references or results.
- Promise major new work without feasibility.

## Disagreement pattern
1. Acknowledge concern.
2. State scope/assumption.
3. Present evidence.
4. Offer clarification revision.

Example:
“We thank the reviewer for this point. Our study focuses on [scope], so [out-of-scope item] is not fully addressed here. To clarify this boundary, we added text in Section X. We also included [new evidence] in Section Y, which supports the revised claim under tested conditions.”
