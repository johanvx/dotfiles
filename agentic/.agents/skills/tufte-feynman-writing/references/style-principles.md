# Style Principles (Tufte/Feynman-Compatible)

## Practical interpretation

1. Clarity over ornament.
2. Evidence and mechanism over adjectives.
3. High information density; low noise.
4. Explain from first principles when possible.
5. Be explicit about assumptions and limits.
6. Conclude with actionable, bounded statements.

## Handling too much context

1. Define one takeaway sentence.
2. Filter notes by relevance to constraints.
3. Keep only top 3–5 points.
4. Convert each point to:
   - Claim
   - Evidence
   - Implication
5. Draft from structure, not raw stream.
6. Compress and polish flow.

## Language habits

Prefer:
- concrete nouns
- active verbs
- measurable terms
- direct transitions

Avoid:
- vague praise
- grand claims without support
- unnecessary abstraction

## Good ending pattern

- “Given [constraint], the practical conclusion is [bounded claim]. The next step is [specific action].”
